2. Course Description

This course will provide an introduction to geographic information systems (GIS) concepts and software. It is intended for undergraduate planning students but open to other undergraduates. The course introduces the concepts of GIS as well as practical training on ESRI's ArcGIS suite, including ArcMap, ArcPy, and other online open-source mapping and visualization tools. Students successfully completing the course will have general familiarity with the major functionality of ArcGIS but will also be introduced at a basic level to other GIS packages, e.g., ArcPy packages, as well as web GIS applications (e.g., ArcGIS Online).

2.1 Topics

The course will cover at a basic level the three most common use cases of GIS – digital cartography, spatial analysis, and spatial data management. Additionally, students should be able to do the following:

  1. Build a solid basis for the critical principles of GIS;
  2. Perform basic spatial analyses such as buffer, overlay, intersection, spatial join, and point-in-polygon analysis;
  3. Apply GIS knowledge and skills to solve real-world problems in urban planning issues:
    • Use geospatial data to create a digital map;
    • Understand common geospatial data formats, including types of vector data, and what kinds of phenomena are appropriate for each type;
    • Integrate data based on location and attributes;
    • Understand the basics of using US Census Bureau data;
    • Create, edit, merge, and manage spatial data layers at a basic level of competency.
  4. Master software by using the help documentation and electronic users’ manuals to find analysis and mapping functions in any GIS software package;
  5. Recognize how geospatial technologies have changed and continue to change our daily lives.

The course will combine lecture and lab sessions. Students will need to plan on having regular access to a copy of ArcGIS 10 by using a local installation or accessing campus computing resources. Course grades are based on completion of software training modules / lab exercises, homework, one exam, one project, and some quizzes.
