2. Course Description

This course will provide an introduction to geographic information systems (GIS) concepts and software. It is intended for undergraduate planning students but open to other undergraduates. The course introduces the concepts of GIS as well as practical training on ESRI's ArcGIS suite. Students successfully completing the course will have general familiarity with the major functionality of ArcGIS but will also be introduced at a basic level to other desktop GIS packages as well as web GIS applications.

2.1 Topics

The course will cover at a basic level the three most common use cases of GIS – digital cartography, spatial analysis, and spatial data management. Additionally, students should be able to do the following:

  1. Use geospatial data to create a digital map with good cartographic style which conveys the intended information clearly and accurately;
  2. Understand common geospatial data formats, including types of vector data, and what kinds of phenomena are appropriate for each type. Understand the concept of geographic scale;
  3. Deal with data that are in different projections and coordinate systems, as well as fix basic problems with the definition of those coordinate systems. Understand which projections are appropriate for which kinds of maps or analysis;
  4. Integrate data based on location and attributes. Perform basic spatial analyses such as buffer, overlay, intersection, spatial join, and point-in-polygon analysis;
  5. Understand the basics of using US Census Bureau data, including how blocks, block groups, and tracts are organized, what information is available at each level, and how to find those data;
  6. Think critically about spatial data and digital maps, including understanding types of error and interpreting commonly produced maps;
  7. Create, edit, merge, and manage spatial data layers at a basic level of competency.

The course will combine lecture and hands-on activities. Students will need to plan on having regular access to a copy of ArcGIS 10 by using a local installation or accessing campus computing resources. Course grades are based on completion of software training modules, one exam, two short projects, and in small part a series of weekly quizzes.

For detail information, please refer to the course schedule.
